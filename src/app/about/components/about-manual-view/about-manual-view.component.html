1. My Players

On this page, you can create your players and modify them whenever you want.

To start, click the button new player

You can set his name and his learned positions and click "create".

The player gets initialized with all skill values set to 100 and an age of 18.

Click edit on the player to adjust his age and skill values and confirm with the check icon. You can also delete created players.

Main position is the position your player is effectively playing on the field. If a player has roles DC and DL, but you mostly play him as DL, set DL as his main position. It will then display the white skills correctly without involving the ones from the other positions.

Effective % is the normalized strength considering only white skills of his main position. These effective white skills are the white ones.

White skill % is the normalized strength considering all white skills. This can differ as soon as a player has more than 1 role. These additional skills are displayed slightly gray.

Top Eleven % is the strength displayed in Top Eleven itself, where all skills are considered.

You can see the differences in calculation in following example, where TopEleven would display a strength of 251%. If you look only at his white skills, it's already 290%. And if you consider that you will play this player only as a DC, he's even up to 333%!

2. My Team

a) Tab "My Players"

This section allows you to calculate the effective strength of the 11 players you put on the field.

You can drag and drop the players up and down. All 11 players above dotted line are included in the caluclated overall strength.

When finishing in the top 8 of the season, just click "NEXT LEVEL", and your player's skills will drop by 20% and their age gets incremented by one year.

Whenever you want, create a snapshot of your team by clicking "CREATE SNAPSHOT". This stores the current skill values of your players in the database for a specific date and will be used in the display of their skill development (see further below).

b) Tab "Development"

Displays the created team snapshots as graph to visualize the players skill development. You can choose if you want to display either of the three types of strengths (as described under "My Players").

Note: if you click "NEXT LEVEL", two snapshots are automatically created; one just before the skill drop, and one right afterwards. This allows you to quickly see the season transitions in the grpah.

Tipp: Create 2-3 snapshots during a season for best results. Also, try to update the skills as accurate as possible before the end of a season.

3. training

a) Tab "Session"

Displays the percentual efficiency of a given training session for each position.

Each panel generally shows which skills are trained. Below, for each position, it shows the affected white skills for that specific position. The percentage is the ratio of position white skills to all trained white skills.
Note: a value of 100% does NOT mean that this session trains ALL white skills, but that all trained skills are white skills of the specific position.
Example: "Pass Go and Shoot" trains 4 skills and has 75% efficiency for MC. That means, that out of the 4 trained skills, 3 are MC white skills. MC might have more white skills, that are not in the scope of "Pass Go and Shoot".

You can filter out desired positions and display just those selected with the filter on top.

b) Tab "Position"

Displays the percentual efficiency of each training session for a given position.

Each panel lists all training sessions, which affect at least one white skill of that position. The training sessions are sorted by most efficient first. The percentage is the ratio of trained white skills to all white skills of that position. The number in brackets is the number of non white skills, which get trained in addition.
Example: "Gym" has 28% and (+1) for GK. This means that "Gym" trains 28% of all GK white skills and furthermore trains 1 skill, that is not a white skill for GK.

You can filter out desired positions and display just those selected with the filter on top.

c) Tab "Skills"

Displays the training sessions which train one ore more selected skills.

You have to select at least one skill to display some data. Once done, it shows every training session that includes the selected skills, as well as all other skills trained by this session.

d) Tab "Drills"

Displays the recommended training sequences provided by Discords bot. You can switch betwenn normal and power training mode.

4. Counter formations

Displays the recommended counter formations provided by Discords bot, separated by mentality (offensive, neutral, defensive). You can also filter by your opponents formation to better access the desired data.
Note: the filter applies only to numbers, all other characters are ignored.
